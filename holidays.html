Major Holidays

🍎

Rosh Hashanah

ראש השנה

Jewish New Year • Tishrei 1-2 • Judgment & Renewal

The "Head of the Year" when we crown God as King and undergo spiritual judgment. Days of introspection, prayer, and the shofar's call to return.

Key Practices: Shofar, sweet foods, Tashlich, intense prayer

🕯️

Yom Kippur

יום כפור

Day of Atonement • Tishrei 10 • Holiest Day

The most sacred day of the year. Complete fast, intense prayer, confession, and atonement. We transcend physical existence to connect with the Divine.

Key Practices: 25-hour fast, Kol Nidre, five prayer services, white garments

🌿

Sukkot

סוכות

Feast of Tabernacles • Tishrei 15-21 • Joy & Unity

"Time of Our Joy" - We dwell in temporary booths commemorating the Divine protection during the Exodus. Celebrate with the Four Species.

Key Practices: Sukkah dwelling, Lulav & Etrog, hospitality, joy

🕎

Chanukah

חנוכה

Festival of Lights • Kislev 25-Tevet 2 • Dedication & Miracles

Commemorates the Maccabee victory and the miraculous eight days of oil. Celebrating Jewish spiritual resistance and the light that defeats darkness.

Key Practices: Menorah lighting, fried foods, dreidel, giving

👑

Purim

פורים

Festival of Lots • Adar 14 • Joy & Hidden Miracles

Celebrating Queen Esther's courage and the salvation from Haman's decree. A day of joy, costumes, and recognizing Divine providence in hidden ways.

Key Practices: Megillah reading, gifts, feasting, charity, joy

🍷

Pesach

פסח

Passover • Nisan 15-22 • Freedom & Redemption

Festival of Freedom commemorating the Exodus from Egypt. We relive the journey from slavery to liberation, from physical to spiritual freedom.

Key Practices: Seder night, matzah, Four Questions, no chametz

📜

Shavuot

שבועות

Feast of Weeks • Sivan 6 • Torah & Revelation

Celebrating the giving of the Torah at Mount Sinai. The marriage between God and Israel, the ultimate purpose of the Exodus.

Key Practices: All-night Torah study, dairy foods, Book of Ruth

💔

Tisha B'Av

תשעה באב

Ninth of Av • Av 9 • Mourning & Reflection

Day of mourning for the destruction of both Temples and other Jewish tragedies. A time for introspection about baseless hatred and national unity.

Key Practices: Fasting, Lamentations, sitting low, mourning customs

🕯️🕯️

Shabbat

שבת

The Sabbath • Every Week • Rest & Holiness

The weekly masterpiece - a taste of the World to Come. Ceasing creative work to recognize God as Creator and celebrating spiritual connection.

Key Practices: Candle lighting, Kiddush, three meals, rest from work
